In the end, I hope to provide you with a well-rounded perspective of who I am, what I'm capable of, and why I would be a fantastic asset to Automattic. Without further ado, I invite you to explore the sections below. Enjoy!

Why I Want to Join You

About a year ago, I decided that I needed a career-change and embarked on a long and interesting journey that included making it to the final interviews for the U.S. Foreign Service, earning my PMP certification, and interviewing a diverse set of industry leaders. After researching Automattic extensively and speaking with Aaron Hohle at length, I knew that I wanted to be an Automattician. Your organizational culture and your employees' warmth, dedication, and competence are inspiring and the TAM position offers unique and exciting opportunities in both the responsibilities and clientele.

The Organization

Automattic's culture, rooted in transparency and integrity, and vision, seeking to extend publishing and commerce tools globally, inspire tens of thousands of people around the world to volunteer to help the movement. In addition, you make people around the world feel empowered in their work and provide them with the autonomy to pursue interesting problems. These attributes alone set Automattic apart from most other technology companies of comparable success and have convinced me that, by working for you, I would be able to make a tangible and positive impact on the world.

The People

As may not surprise you, both your employees and volunteers are prolific and candid in their descriptions of life as an Automattician. Much of what I have learned has come from the WordPress slack instance, community forums, and individual blogs. I've also thoroughly enjoyed Matt's podcast, Distributed, and learning about every facet of distributed work. What is immediately clear is that Automattic honors and empowers its people and the community members care deeply about each other. Who wouldn't want to be a part of that?

The Position

On top of joining a great organization and getting to work with intelligent, driven, and empathetic colleagues, the TechnicalAccount Manager position offers an exciting set of clients and responsibilities. I thrive under pressure and purpose and would \relish the opportunity to collaborate with business leaders around the world on complex, interesting problems make me vibrate with excitement. It's also clear that work will never be linear or stale as every debug, expanded functionality, and site redesign is unique and brings with the opportunity to learn and grow. That combination: incredible clients, engaging problems, and continual learning, is exactly what I want in a career.

Why I Am a Good Fit

At first glance, my experience in software development, geology, and academic research suggests that I am primarily a researcher and engineer (not a bad thing!). While my strong technical background is undeniable, it does not define me. For the past 14 years, I've thrived in a diverse set of roles that have provided me with extensive management experience, honed my interpersonal skills, and made me an amiable and reliable colleague. Automattic's combination of highly collaborative work and a globally dispersed workforce makes these skills essential tools for every employee.

Project Management

Fresh out of highschool, I started as the manager of a research lab at UC San Diego, where I built a student worker program and hired, trained, and supervised a 5 to 8-person team. I fostered a culture of creativity, transparency, and equity that empowered employees to improvise process improvements and bound us together as a team with common purpose. It was incredible how, as the "shy kid" just months prior, I found this ability to connect with others and make a tangible impact on their lives.

Emotional Intelligence

In college, while completing dual technical degrees, I continued my managerial and leadership training as a resident advisor. The responsibility I enjoyed most was organizing social events that exposed my residents to new activities and ideas and provided them a fun, safe environment to mingle and bond. The most impactful parts, however, were the times I mentored residents through hardship, intervened in conflicts, and managed community emergencies. Through these experiences, I discovered how to listen, how to calm, and how to lead my team and community members.

Team Player

As both a graduate student and a postdoctoral fellow, the success of my research has been dependent upon continuous collaboration with my global colleagues. I've always had a small, core team of engineers, technicians, and fellow researchers with whom I worked constantly. However, all labs have limited capabilities, so I occasionally reached out to external experts to develop partnerships, use on their equipment, and get their advice on my work. My individual projects were also part of larger research portfolios, which required that my work align with the overall strategic goals. The sum result was a complex web of people and institutions maintained by monitoring foundational relationships and dependably communicating progress.

My Unique Skills

I am enticed by the Technical Account Manager position because it offers a mix of customer interaction, strategic leadership, and problem solving. My success will depend heavily on my ability to communicate effectively across functions, build and manage client relationships, and deliver elegant solutions. This is a tall order, by any standards, but my past experience has prepared me well and I can't wait to contribute.

Communication

My doctoral advisor was unique in his emphasis on his students' communication skills, particularly technical writing, oral presentation, and active listening. I spent countless hours pouring over my manuscripts, seeking to clarify my message and condense my thoughts. I delivered dozens of compelling presentations to diverse audiences, big and small, by distilling years of work into 20-30 minutes. Most importantly, however, I met with key stakeholders and external experts, asking good questions, absorbing their responses, and acting on their advice. These power skills have proven essential to both my professional and personal growth.

International Experience

My move to Taiwan in 2018 was life-changing. I've traveled abroad fairly extensively, but the contrast between being a visitor and being a resident is stark. My time abroad has made me appreciate that local cultures are persistent and it is my responsibility to adjust, in both life and work, if I am to thrive. I am more resilient, culturally-sensitive, and patient towards my environment as a result.

For example, while being trainsed by a doctoral student in my Taiwanese research team, I noticed that he was uncomfortable giving me direct, critical feedback. I attributed his behavior to a culturally-inclined deferrence to my perceived authority (as the "higher-educated" person), but found that it hindered my progress and ability to contribute. In response, I started watching for covert signs of discontent and asking additional clarifying questions. This slight behavioral change not only accelerated my learning, but also led to me developing the autonomous data-processing tool that became my first contribution to the team. Through a little sensitivity and flexiblity, I was able to vastly improve our communication and my efficacy.

Technical Problem Solving

I've spent the past 10 years of my career learning new, highly-technical skills. I entered my graduate program knowing nothing about my research field or the experimental methods I would soon master. In a relatively short time, I accumulated a collection of skills (e.g. hardware engineering, mathematical modeling, 3D data analysis and visualization) uniquely tuned to my research needs and became an expert in my field. I then repeated the process after moving to Taiwan, as I changed the focus of my research completely and, thus, started from scratch. Through these experiences, I've perfected not only how to learn from others, but also how to independently teach myself and be productive in ambiguous environments.

At a finer scale, my learning experiences are typically linked to a complex problem that I overcame using increasingly elegant solutions. Since I was a kid disassembling old radios and VCR players, I have been interested in how things work. That curiousity morphed with age into how I could build something and, eventually, into how I could prefect it. This mentality persists to this day and motivates me as I approach any problem, whether debugging code, troubleshooting an experiment, or detangling complex data. My need to find the right solution has been critical to my success by driving me to question, experiment, and iterate continuously.

How I Will Contribute

The Client-Engineer Interface

My primary contribution to WordPress VIP comes with my ability to communicate cross-functionally and build long-term, positive relationships. I have a strong technical background and have already learned so much about WordPress development independently, but my strength is translating between the technical and non-technical. I will work with our clients to define their vision and requirements and then turn to our engineers to collaborate on potential solutions. By communicating clearly, I will ensure success and impact of these projects, which will further tighten the bond between WordPress VIP and its clients.

Project Management

As both a graduate student and a postdoctoral fellow, I brought long-term (3-4 years), high-budget research projects to completion. I proved adept at disaggregating complex requirements into smaller, digestible components and then dedicating myself to getting the project done. These projects were highly complex and had inherent risks that could only be mitigated by careful attention and coordination. Effective and agile project management is becoming essential to growth and resilience in diverse economic sectors. As a certified project manager, I look forward to tackling your most complex projects and helping them succeed.

Strategic Location

On a practical note, my being located in the APAC timezone and particularly in Taiwan brings with it three key strategic benefits. First, as far as I can discern, less than 15% of Automatticians reside in this timezone and only a handful are VIP Technical Account Managers. I would prove a critical point of contact for VIP's clients in the US and Europe outside business hours, improving Automattic's sterling customer service. Second, the APAC region is the fasting growing economic zone in the world with significant opportunities to expand the adoption of WordPress VIP and other services. Taiwan is strategically located, with ready access to all countries, and would prove to be an important operational base. Finally, Taiwan itself hosts a fledgling WordPress community, but has incredible technical talent just waiting to be tapped into. I would gladly work with local Automatticians and WordPress contributors to recruit top talent as both volunteers and employees.
